Reflections - WiFi

Petri Ojala, Apr 5, 2021, Network Wifi

Upgrading WiFi is more complex topic. While there’s only two devices in the household with WiFi 6 (802.11ax) yet, one phone and Macbook Pro M1, the newer WiFi access points also tend to improve existing WiFi performance and coverage – at least they should..

For WiFi the goal is rather simple – to be able to use the full gigabit Internet bandwidth over WiFi with a suitable client. Back in 2017 I switched from Apple’s Airport Airport Extreme to TP-Link Archer C5400 access point and with iMac 5K it was possible to do so. C5400 was a bit pain to configure to be as dumb as possible but it was doable and it has been pretty much troublefree.

C5400 has been pretty decent device with one 2.4 GHz and two 5 GHz radios, all on the same SID with client steering and support for up to 160 Mhz bandwidth. A year ago I also tested Netgear’s RAX80, a 8-stream WiFi 6 model, but despite the better specs it didn’t perform any better.

So what should be the plan?

First of wall, it’s hard to know in advance what kind of access point setup the new apartment will require. It might work with a single access point in the center somewhere, or it might require more than one. I want to keep the setup simple, the access point should be “install and forget” and just bridge between wireless and wired network. If I can live without a controller, that would be optimal.

Any new WiFi access point should include WiFi 6 but also WiFi 6E as 6 GHz 802.11ax will be more common over the next years and should be relatively similar to 5 GHz in coverage but it has a lot of bandwidth available for clients.

There are some interesting WiFi 6E access points coming to the market like TP-Link’s AX96 (AX7800) https://www.tp-link.com/us/home-networking/wifi-router/archer-ax96/ with three radios, one for 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz and 6 GHz. Ubiquiti’s Unifi is probably out of question due to the recent drama but perhaps TP-Link’s Omada system will include similar radio support as AX96.

I think it’s safe to continue with the current access point, learn the new characteristics first before making any moves. Hopefully there will be interesting new devices on the market as well.
